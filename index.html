Classic Nigerian Egusi Soup

A rich and savory West African soup made from ground melon seeds, vegetables, and assorted meats — perfect with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 50 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 500g assorted meat (beef, goat meat, or chicken)
  • 2 smoked fish, cleaned and deboned
  • 1 cup stockfish, soaked
  • 2 cooking spoons red palm oil
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 3 cups chopped ugu (fluted pumpkin leaves) or spinach
  • 2 tablespoons ground pepper or to taste
  • 4 cups meat stock
  • Salt and seasoning cubes, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season and boil the assorted meats until tender; set aside, reserving the stock.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot on medium heat.
  3. Add ground egusi and fry for 3–5 minutes, stirring continuously to avoid burning.
  4. Pour in the meat stock and stir until the egusi thickens into a paste-like consistency.
  5. Add the cooked meat, smoked fish, and stockfish into the pot.
  6. Season with ground crayfish, pepper, salt, and seasoning cubes; stir well.
  7. Allow the soup to cook on low heat for 10–15 minutes.
  8. Stir in the chopped vegetables, simmer for 3–5 minutes, and remove from heat.

Tips

Tip: For extra flavor, add some locust beans (iru) when frying the egusi paste.
